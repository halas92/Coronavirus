    T

    he novel Coronavirus now known as COVID-19

    originated in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. Officials scrambled to implement countermeasures against the disease's contagion and lethality, and within weeks, the coronavirus made its way to the United States. A Washington state man who had been travelling to China became the nation's first reported case Jan. 21, 2020. From there, reports of infection continued to rise throughout the country.

    As of March 21, there are 182,864 reported active cases of coronavirus worldwide. The virus has caused 11,811 deaths globally, and wrought havoc in countries like Italy and Iran, where cases number in the tens of thousands. There is no vaccine for COVID-19 currently in circulation, and experimental products are likely to undergo months of testing before being cleared for mass distribution.

    Maryland reported its first three cases—all of them residents of Montgomery County—on March 5.

    Health experts and government officials began calling for increased social distancing measures to contain the spread of the virus as cases continued to rise. The World Health Organization formally declared the coronavirus a pandemic March 11. In Maryland, the General Assembly was forced to adjourn early, amid concerns the legislative session was continuing without "the full voice and input of the people of Maryland."

    Data show a rapid increase in the number of positive reported cases of coronavirus in Maryland since it first appeared in the state. Maryland reported its first death from COVID-19—a Prince George's County man in his 60s—March 18. Gov. Larry Hogan announced that a Baltimore County man in his 60s was the state's second confirmed COVID-19 death Friday evening.
